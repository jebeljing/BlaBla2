Men Buy More From Manly Men

Men buy more from manly men. Professor Otterbring defends the research. Interestingly, in later research we found that the effect was even greater for male customers of short stature. We think this is because the physically fit male we used activated the classic male competitive instinct. Shorter men just don't seem to trigger the same evolutionary urge to show off. Research has shown that women respond to intrasexual competition, too Images of stronger-looking guys caused men to prefer larger logos on clothing. This effect was driven by the shorter male men, not the taller ones. If you'are a company selling status-signaling luxury goods-like cars, watches, and clothes-it's certainly an idea to consider. However, the tall, big person doesn't have to be an employee or even physically present. Photos also work. We see implications for not just retail hiring but also adversing and marketing. Manly men appear to influence short male shoppers the most. Though the psychological mechanism is an increased drive to compete with people of the same gender, retailers will have to decide whether it's a good strategic move. It could drive the sales in the short run. But it might also cause the short man to leave the store feeling unhappy and not come back. Be aware of these superficial biases and understand how heavily they can influence consumption.
